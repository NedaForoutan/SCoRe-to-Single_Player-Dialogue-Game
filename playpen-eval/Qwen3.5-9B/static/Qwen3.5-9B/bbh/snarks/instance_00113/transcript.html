In reality, small companies often struggle with resources, so having terrible support is a plausible, albeit unfortunate, outcome. However, the phrasing "Yeah, obviously... because they are just tiny indie company" uses the word "obviously" to mock the listener's assumption that small size automatically equals bad service, or it ironically suggests that being small is a *reason* for the failure when the listener might expect the opposite (or simply highlights the absurdity of the situation).

More importantly, look at option (B): "Yeah, obviously that **megaconglomerate** has terrible support because they are just **tiny indie company**." This sentence contains a direct logical contradiction. A "megaconglomerate" cannot be a "tiny indie company." This makes option (B) nonsensical rather than sarcastic.

The structure "Yeah, obviously [negative outcome] because [expected cause]" is a classic sarcastic trope used to highlight the gap between expectation and reality.
